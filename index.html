Hire Me Tho

I had the unique burden of really supportive parents, you know how artists can be. I spent most of my life juggling fantasies about being Indiana Jones or Michael Jackson. So cool. So different. So me? So I took the scenic route. Inevitably I ended up with a pricey academic hobby and a penchant for R&B.
In a shocking turn of events, I received my B.A. in Anthropology and played music in two bands for most of my twenties. It went well - ish. We opened up for several... two national acts including Walk the Moon (RCA) and the Idle Hands. They opened up for us but hey, we paid 'em. We also put more than 30 separate syncs on tv and then just jumped right into a legal battle. super tight.

So as the sun began to set on my twenties, I pulled an audible.

I needed something reliable but I needed something like music, that I could be better at in the end. The same orchestration. The art and the order. The parts and their functionality. Systems, systems generally are something to be curious about, and I am, about the complexity of these systems that surround us everyday, unnoticed, unexamined. Let's face it that's what I am - life is - you are. Don't forget it. There's something beautiful and strange happening here people. Of all things, computer programming prompted this same fervent curiosity. This excitement. Simply put, it was the perfect union between technical proficiency and creative intuition AND get this, they pay you a salary. I think I just heard my parents exhale.

So, uh, life's short. Wear a lot of hats, Mike and Indie did.

I run a small Web Development and Design Boutique. It's tight.
